NordBrowser — Beta Test Scope Review Report

Version: 0.1  |  Date: April 2026  |  Prepared by: Mert Fatih Simsek  |  Beta access via: NordVPN

1. Executive Summary

NordBrowser is a VPN-integrated browser solution currently under beta evaluation. It is developed under a NordVPN partnership, which has granted early access for internal testing. The core value proposition is distributing a fully VPN-enabled browser to employees without requiring individual VPN client installations or configuration — significantly reducing IT overhead and support burden.

This report summarizes the initial test pass conducted as of March 30, 2026, covering Security Policy controls, Routing Policy enforcement, and peripheral device access. Results are categorized as working or not working based on observed behavior.

2. Background & Business Rationale

The organization currently relies on NordVPN for secure remote access. Distributing and maintaining VPN clients across all employee endpoints involves ongoing operational complexity: installation, configuration, credential management, and troubleshooting.

NordBrowser addresses this by embedding VPN connectivity directly into a managed browser. Key benefits identified:

  • Eliminates per-device VPN client installation and configuration
  • Reduces IT support burden for VPN-related issues
  • Centralized control over browser-level security policies
  • Granular routing rules to enforce VPN access for specific destinations
  • Faster onboarding of new employees requiring secure web access

3. Test Scope & Methodology

Testing was performed on a controlled internal environment using beta credentials from NordVPN. The following capability areas were included:

  • Security Policy management — ability to restrict or permit browser-level features for end users
  • Routing Policy enforcement — ability to mandate VPN tunnel usage for specific domains or destinations
  • Peripheral access controls — microphone and camera handling within the managed browser

Tests were executed manually. Each feature was tested by enabling/disabling the relevant policy and verifying the resulting browser behavior. No automated test framework was used in this phase.

4.2 Routing Policy

The Routing Policy feature allows administrators to define specific domains or URLs that must be accessed exclusively through the VPN tunnel, regardless of the user's general network state.

Routing policy is particularly valuable for enforcing compliance on sensitive internal or business-critical services without requiring users to manage VPN state manually.

6. Recommendations

  1. Report microphone and camera policy failures to NordVPN beta support with detailed reproduction steps and environment specs
  2. Expand routing policy testing to cover at least 5–10 domains, including internal business applications
  3. Validate behavior when the VPN connection drops mid-session (failsafe / kill-switch behavior)
  4. Define and document which Security Policy settings are mandatory vs. optional for the organization's deployment
  5. Request a policy audit log from NordVPN to ensure administrator actions are traceable
  6. Conduct a second test pass after NordVPN addresses the peripheral policy issues

8. Conclusion

NordBrowser demonstrates strong potential as a zero-friction VPN browser solution for enterprise use. Core capabilities — Security Policy controls for file operations and clipboard, and Routing Policy enforcement for domain-level VPN requirements — are functional and meet initial expectations.

Two high-priority defects related to microphone and camera access controls must be resolved before the solution can be considered production-ready. Pending these fixes and additional testing coverage, NordBrowser appears to be a viable candidate for replacing individual VPN client deployments across the organization.

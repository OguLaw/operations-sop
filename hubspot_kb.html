Getting Started: Setting Up Your HubSpot Account

Welcome to HubSpot! Before you dive in, complete these setup steps to ensure your workspace is personalized, secure, and ready to use.

Step 1 — Accept Your Invitation

You'll receive an email from HubSpot: "You've been invited to join Oğuz Law on HubSpot." Click Accept Invitation and follow the signup prompts.

Step 2 — Fill In Your Basic Info

Enter your name, company email, and a secure password. Your CRM Specialist will then fill in your Job Title, Manager, working hours, and timezone.

Step 3 — Set Your Preferences

Default Page: Choose the page you want to land on when you log in (e.g. Deals, Tasks, Contacts). Navigate to Settings → General → Profile.

Settings → General → Profile: Default Home Page set to Dashboards

Task Preferences: Configure how tasks are created and displayed. Navigate to Settings → General → Tasks.

Settings → General → Tasks: due date defaults (1 business day, 8:00 AM) and follow-up task preferences

Timezone & Date Format: Confirm it's set to your location and format.

Settings → General: Language (English) and Date/Time format (United States)

Step 4 — Add a Trusted Phone Number

Go to Settings → Security → Phone Number. Enter your trusted phone number — this is used for security alerts and password recovery only, never for sales.

Settings → Security: Trusted Phone Number — add a number for identity verification

Step 5 — Set Up Passkeys (Recommended)

Passkeys are a safer, password-free way to log in using Face ID, fingerprint, or a secure device.

Settings → Security → Passkeys: Set up biometric login

Step 6 — Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA is required for all team members. Go to Settings → Security → Two-Factor Authentication, choose SMS or an authenticator app, and complete verification.

Settings → Security → 2FA: Remove Security App / Set up secondary method / View backup codes

Contact Page Views

The Contacts page lets you work with saved Views — combinations of filters and column setups that help you manage your pipeline without re-applying filters every time.

Go to CRM → Contacts → All Views to browse available views. You can see your own views, team-shared views, and admin-created defaults. Drag views to reorder them, or click Add Views to show more.

To create a custom view: click Add View → Create new view, set your filters (lifecycle stage, lead status, owner, last activity, etc.), choose visibility (private / team / everyone), then Save.

To edit columns: open a view, click Actions → Edit columns, and drag/drop to reorder. Changes only apply to your view unless you're editing a shared one.

Default Views (Admin-Created)

  • No Activity Logged — Contacts with Lead Status = New Lead and no logged engagement. Review and reach out, or update the lead status.
  • Activity Logged but Still New Lead — At least one activity logged, but lead status is still "New Lead." Update it to reflect the actual progress.

Overview Tab

The Overview tab gives a compact summary of the most critical data: pinned activities, upcoming actions, recent activities, Data Highlights (conditional properties), and embedded reports.

Conditional Properties — some fields only appear based on specific conditions (e.g. when lead status is Unqualified or Not Moving Forward). Once filled, they appear in the Data Highlights section and can be updated from both the left sidebar and the Overview panel.

  • Referred In (Other) — how the contact was referred to us
  • Referred Out — where the contact was referred to
  • Unqualified Reason — only appears when Lead Status = Unqualified
  • Loss Reason — only appears when Lead Status = Not Moving Forward

Lead Management Process

The full process for tracking, managing, and converting leads from first contact to active client.

New Lead Creation

  • Create the contact during or before the call — this ensures OpenPhone logs the recorded call to the new contact automatically.
  • You can create a contact with just a phone number first, then fill in all other details after the call.
  • Always set Lead Status to New Lead immediately on creation (or reload the page — it sets automatically).

Always collect: full name, email, phone number (double-check — people can have multiple numbers), where they found us, and whether they've contacted the company before.

Assigning the Contact Owner

Assign the Contact Owner (usually the team leader) immediately. The Contact Owner is responsible for reaching out. If someone else is asked to reach out instead, update the contact owner to that person — otherwise, automated tasks will go to the wrong person.

Calling Leads & Lead Status Updates

  • Call unanswered: Set Lead Status to "Attempted to Contact." Create follow-up tasks for 1 day, 2 days, 3 days, and 7 days. After all attempts with no answer, set to "No Answer."
  • Call answered — unqualified: Set Lead Status to "Unqualified" and select an Unqualified Reason.
  • Call answered — qualified: Set Lead Status to "In Progress" and Lifecycle Stage to "Sales Qualified Lead."
  • Lead qualified but not yet persuaded: Set to "Long Term Follow Up" and create a future task.

Deal Process

  • When a deal is sent: Create a deal in HubSpot linked to the contact. Set Deal Stage to "Contract Sent." Update the Contact Card Lead Status to "Open Deal."
  • Deal accepted: Change Deal Stage to "Closed Won." Update Lead Status to "Active Client." HubSpot automatically updates Lifecycle Stage to "Customer."
  • Deal rejected: Change Deal Stage to "Closed Lost." Select Closed Lost Reason. Update Lead Status to "Not Moving Forward" → choose Loss Reason → then update to "Long-Term Follow-Up" and set a re-engagement task.

Integrations

Connect Your Email to HubSpot

Go to Settings → General → Email → Connect Personal Email. Choose your provider (Gmail, Outlook), log in, and allow permissions. Enable Log emails and Track emails.

After connecting: emails sent from HubSpot or Gmail are logged automatically. If you send from Missive, you must manually log it: go to your Gmail Sent folder and click "Log email to HubSpot." If you don't do this, the email won't appear on the contact's timeline.

Integrate Your Email Signature

Go to Settings → General → Email → Manage Email Signatures → HTML. In Gmail, open a Sent email with your signature → right-click inspect → find the signature element → copy outerHTML → paste into HubSpot. Check that emojis render correctly.

CRM Ticket System

Submit a ticket for: questions about a property or feature, system issues, adding a new user, requesting a new custom property, or suggesting improvements.

Navigate to CRM → Tickets → Create Ticket. Select the "CRM Pipeline (Reach out to Emel for HubSpot Inquiries)" pipeline. Fill in: Ticket Name, Ticket Type, Ticket Priority, Owner of Request (yourself), Ticket Status = "New CRM Ticket," Ticket Description (detailed), Ticket Owner = Emel Gedik. Associate with a contact if relevant.

Repeat Clients — CRM Update Strategy

If a person reaches out again for a new service, use their existing Contact record — do not create a new contact.

  • If the new inquiry is not a fit: Set Lead Status = Unqualified, fill Unqualified Reason.
  • If they previously signed a contract: Keep Lifecycle Stage = Customer. Do not move them back to Lead or MQL.
  • Log all communication in the Activity Timeline.
  • Add tags: Repeat Client, Multiple Inquiries.
  • Create a follow-up task if needed.

Example note: "Reached out again on 25.04.2025 for a B-2 visa. Not eligible based on current criteria."

Referral Companies in HubSpot

Referral company representatives are added to HubSpot without team permissions. This prevents them from seeing company-wide notes, calls, and emails — protecting client confidentiality.

How It Works

  1. When a contact is assigned to a referral rep, an automated pinned note is created on the contact record.
  2. Internal staff copy-paste relevant call summaries, emails, and notes into the comments section of that note.
  3. Tag the referral rep with @ so they are notified.
  4. Referral reps can only see that note and its comments — not any other activities.
⚠️ Do not add referral partners to any team — doing so would expose all company-wide notes and activities to them.

When a Referral Company Refers a Contact to Us

  1. The referral rep creates the contact and is the initial owner.
  2. The automated note appears — they add their context in comments.
  3. They set themselves as Secondary Contact Owner first.
  4. Then they change Contact Owner to Hassan Kadak (Intake Leader).

Important: if they skip step 3 and change the owner first, they lose access to the contact. Remind them to always set themselves as Secondary Contact Owner before transferring.

Case Type Properties

Case type–specific properties appear dynamically in HubSpot based on the selected Case Type (e.g. H-1B, E-2, EB-1C). They capture key pre-sales information in a structured format — reducing the need for free-text notes.

When you select a Case Type, the relevant property set loads automatically. Fill in all visible fields instead of writing the same information manually in Pre-Sales Notes.

Name Format & Consistency Requirement

The deal name and the contact's full name must be identical. This ensures proper matching in both HubSpot and MyCase.

  • Do NOT use generic terms like "Asylum Case" — always specify the exact type.
  • Always use English characters only. No Turkish special characters (ç, ş, ğ, ö, ü, ı → use c, s, g, o, u, i).
  • Deal name and Contact name must match exactly. "Ali Kizil" ≠ "Ali Ahmet Kizil."
